## Executive Summary

**Limited** is a self-custodial financial platform offering stablecoin-based business banking with Visa cards, targeting international entrepreneurs and crypto-native companies.

**Key Opportunity:** Unique positioning at intersection of:
- Self-custody (crypto-native trust)
- Business banking features (team management, cards)
- Global accessibility (not constrained by traditional banking)

**Market Size:**
- Global remittances: $700B+ annually
- SMB banking: $2T+ addressable market
- Stablecoin market: $180B+ (growing 40% YoY)

# PART 1: USE CASES

---

## Use Case #1: Cross-Border Business Operations

**Scenario:** Software agency with 15 remote contractors across 8 countries

**Problem:**
- Wire transfers cost $30-50 each + 3-5 day delays
- Managing multiple currencies creates FX losses
- Traditional banks reject crypto-adjacent businesses

**Limited Solution:**
- Pay all contractors in USDC (instant, free between Limited users)
- Single treasury view across all currencies
- Self-custody reduces bank relationship risk

**Annual Savings:** ~$15,000 in fees + $25,000 in FX optimization

---

## Use Case #2: Crypto-Native Treasury Management

**Scenario:** NFT marketplace with $2M operational runway

**Problem:**
- Coinbase doesn't offer business cards
- Moving crypto to traditional bank triggers taxable events
- Need to pay AWS, Vercel, employee salaries in fiat

**Limited Solution:**
- Hold treasury in USDC (avoid volatility)
- Issue cards to team for SaaS subscriptions
- Seamless crypto-to-fiat bridge without selling positions

**Benefit:** Maintain crypto-native operations while accessing traditional payment rails

---

## Use Case #3: Emerging Market Entrepreneurs

**Scenario:** Argentine SaaS founder earning $50K/month in USD

**Problem:**

**Limited Solution:**

**Value Preservation:** Save ~$70K annually from currency depreciation

**What It Means:**
- Users maintain control of private keys (vs. traditional banks holding funds)
- Funds can't be frozen by company decision
- Transparent on-chain reserves

**Why It Matters:**
- Post-FTX trust crisis: "Not your keys, not your coins"
- Appeals to crypto-native segment
- Regulatory moat (if positioned correctly)

**Competitive Advantage:**
- Wise, Revolut, Mercury = custodial (they control your money)
- Limited = self-custody (you control your money)

---

## Forte #2: Real-Time Settlement

**What It Means:**
- Stablecoin transfers settle in seconds/minutes
- No 3-5 day wire transfer delays
- 24/7/365 operations (no banking hours)

**Why It Matters:**
- Working capital efficiency (don't wait for funds)
- Better cash flow management
- Competitive edge for time-sensitive payments

**Example:**
- Traditional wire: Friday → arrives Monday
- Limited USDC: Friday 11pm → arrives Friday 11:02pm

# PART 3: WEAKNESSES

---

## Weakness #1: Regulatory Uncertainty

**The Issue:**
- "Not a bank" = unclear consumer protections
- Regulatory landscape evolving rapidly
- Potential for sudden rule changes

**Risks:**
- MiCA in Europe, potential US stablecoin legislation
- Could require expensive licensing
- Worst case: Forced shutdown in certain jurisdictions

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Work with regulators proactively (don't wait for enforcement)
- Partner with licensed banks (hybrid model)
- Geo-diversification (don't depend on single market)
- Build compliance-first culture from day one

---

## Weakness #2: Stablecoin Risk

**The Issue:**
- Dependent on USDC (Circle) and EURC
- If Circle fails or is regulated out of existence, platform breaks
- Depeg risk (see UST collapse)

**Specific Risks:**
- Circle's reserves: Are they actually 1:1?
- Regulatory action against Circle
- Bank run scenario (everyone redeems at once)

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Multi-stablecoin support (add USDT, DAI, PYUSD)
- Proof-of-reserves transparency
- Insurance fund for depeg scenarios
- Gradual move to decentralized stablecoins (DAI, GHO)

---

## Weakness #3: Limited Fiat Support

**The Issue:**
- Only USD and EUR stablecoins
- No GBP, JPY, AUD, CAD, etc.
- Limits addressability in key markets (UK, Japan, Australia)

**Impact:**
- UK businesses want GBP accounts
- Can't compete with Wise (50+ currencies)
- FX conversion required even with Limited

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Short-term: Competitive FX conversion rates
- Medium-term: Add GBPT (Paxos GBP stablecoin)
- Long-term: Partner with local stablecoin issuers in each region

---

## Weakness #4: Invite-Only Growth Model

**The Issue:**
- Slow customer acquisition
- Competitors can outpace growth
- Limits network effects

**Why They're Doing It:**
- Controlled scaling (reduce operational risk)
- Regulatory compliance (easier with smaller user base)
- Prestige/exclusivity marketing

**Trade-off:**
- Benefit: Higher-quality customers, lower churn
- Cost: Slower path to market dominance

**Recommendation:**
- Move to open signup after 1,000 business accounts
- Maintain invite bonuses for referrals

---

## Weakness #5: No Deposit Insurance

**The Issue:**
- Traditional banks: FDIC insurance ($250K per account)
- Limited: No equivalent protection
- If Limited fails, funds could be at risk

**Customer Perception:**
- "Less safe than a real bank"
- Institutional customers may be hesitant

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Self-custody messaging: "You own the keys, we can't lose your money"
- Third-party audits (quarterly proof-of-reserves)
- Partner insurance (some crypto custodians offer coverage)
- Education: FDIC protects against bank failure, not government seizure

---

## Weakness #6: Variable Fee Structure

**The Issue:**
- Pricing not transparent on website
- "Varies by destination and amount"
- Harder to compare vs. competitors

**Customer Friction:**
- Can't calculate costs before signing up
- Uncertainty = hesitation
- Competitors (Wise) win on transparency

**Recommendation:**
- Publish fee calculator on website
- Standard pricing tiers (remove variability)
- Price anchor: "50% cheaper than banks" with examples

---

## Weakness #7: Crypto Volatility Perception

**The Issue:**
- Mainstream businesses scared of "crypto"
- Even though stablecoins are stable
- Association with FTX, Luna, etc.

**Marketing Challenge:**
- "Stablecoin banking" sounds risky to normies
- Need to educate on USDC ≠ Bitcoin

**Rebranding Strategy:**
- Emphasize "digital dollars" over "crypto"
- "Dollar accounts, internet-speed transfers"
- Position as fintech, not crypto

---

## Weakness #8: Compliance Burden

**The Issue:**
- Users must track stablecoin transactions for tax
- In many countries, crypto-to-crypto = taxable event
- Accounting complexity

**Example:**
- US: Every USDC → USD conversion = potential capital gain/loss
- Nightmare for bookkeeping

**Mitigation:**
- Built-in tax reporting (Form 1099, etc.)
- Partnerships with crypto accounting software (Bitwave, Cryptio)
- Automatic cost-basis tracking

## Self-Custody Implementation

### Option 1: MPC (Multi-Party Computation)

**How It Works:**
- Private key split across multiple parties
- Signatures computed without reconstructing key
- No single point of failure

**Pros:**
- User doesn't manage seed phrases
- Fast transaction signing
- Better UX

**Cons:**
- Complex cryptography
- Vendor lock-in
- Still requires trust

**Vendors:**
- Fireblocks (~$100K+/year)
- Qredo (mid-market)
- Sepior (on-premise)

**Best For:** Limited's mainstream users

---

### Option 2: Smart Contract Wallets

**How It Works:**
- Wallets are smart contracts (ERC-4337)
- Programmable logic
- Social recovery

**Pros:**
- True self-custody
- No seed phrases needed
- Programmable spending limits
- Multi-sig for businesses

**Cons:**
- Only EVM chains
- Higher transaction costs
- Newer technology

**Solutions:**
- Safe (Gnosis Safe) - $100B+ secured
- ZeroDev - AA toolkit
- Biconomy - gas abstraction

**Best For:** Crypto-native power users

---

### Recommended: Hybrid Approach

**Personal Accounts:**
- Smart Contract Wallet (Safe)
- User owns keys
- Social recovery
- Full control

**Business Accounts:**
- MPC Wallet (Fireblocks)
- Team-based access
- Admin controls
- Faster operations

**Why Hybrid:**
- Personal users get true self-custody
- Business users get team management
- Flexibility for different needs

## Card Issuance Flow

```
1. User loads USDC to Limited wallet
        ↓
2. Limited locks USDC as collateral
        ↓
3. Marqeta issues Visa card
        ↓
4. User spends $100 at merchant
        ↓
5. Merchant charges in USD
        ↓
6. Limited converts $100 USDC to fiat
        ↓
7. Settlement via banking partner
        ↓
8. User's USDC balance -= $100
```

**3. Hybrid Approach:**
- Small tx (<$100): L2
- Large tx (>$100): Ethereum mainnet
- Internal tx: Off-chain

## Off-Chain Settlement

### For Internal Limited-to-Limited Transfers

```
User A sends $100 USDC to User B
        ↓
No blockchain transaction!
        ↓
Update database:
  userA.balance -= 100
  userB.balance += 100
        ↓
Instant, zero fees
        ↓
Only settle on-chain when withdrawing externally
```

**Benefits:**
- Instant settlement (no block confirmations)
- Zero gas fees
- Better UX
- Reduces blockchain load

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Daily on-chain reconciliation
- Merkle tree proof-of-reserves
- Quarterly third-party audits
- 1:1 backing guarantee

## Risk #2: Stablecoin Depeg

**Scenario:** USDC loses $1 peg (like UST collapse)

**Probability:** Low (10%)

**Impact:** High (bank run, customer losses)

**Mitigation:**
- Multi-stablecoin support (USDC, USDT, DAI)
- Real-time monitoring and alerts
- Insurance fund (1-2% of deposits)
- Automatic circuit breakers

## Risk #3: Security Breach

**Scenario:** Hack of MPC provider or smart contracts

**Probability:** Low (5%)

**Impact:** Catastrophic (customer fund loss)

**Mitigation:**
- Multi-vendor approach (not single MPC provider)
- Insurance coverage ($100M+ cyber policy)
- Bug bounties
- Regular audits (quarterly smart contract audits)
- Cold storage for majority of funds
